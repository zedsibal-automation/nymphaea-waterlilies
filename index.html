Our Story

Grown with love,
picked at their best

NYMPHAEA Waterlilies began with a simple devotion: to raise water lilies the way nature intended — slowly, attentively, and with genuine care for every bloom. From our nursery in Lawy, Capas, Tarlac, every plant is nurtured from tuber to flower before it ever reaches your pond.

We work with hardy and tropical day-blooming varieties, selecting only the healthiest divisions to share with fellow water gardeners — because a pond full of colour makes everything feel a little more alive.

FAQ

Common Questions

Place the tuber in a planting basket with heavy aquatic soil, angled slightly upward, then lower it into the pond so the growing tip sits 6–12 inches below the water surface. Raise it closer to the surface as new leaves develop.

Most varieties bloom best with at least 5–6 hours of direct sunlight a day. Less light won't harm the plant, but flowering will be sparser.

Feed with an aquatic plant fertilizer tablet every 3–4 weeks during the growing season, tucked into the soil near the roots rather than dissolved in the water.

Hardy varieties can overwinter outdoors as long as the tuber sits below the pond's freeze line. Tropical varieties should be brought indoors or treated as seasonal in colder climates.
